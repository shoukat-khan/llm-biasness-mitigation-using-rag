Employment discrimination based on gender is a form of bias that limits both individual potential and organizational success. When workplaces eliminate gender bias in hiring, promotion, and compensation, they benefit from access to the full talent pool and improved performance.

Despite efforts to recruit and retain diverse talent, gender disparities often start small but compound over time through biased evaluation, unequal opportunities for advancement, and workplace cultures that favor one gender. Addressing these systemic issues requires conscious effort and structural change.
